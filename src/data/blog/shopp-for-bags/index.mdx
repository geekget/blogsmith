These days, going to a stadium sporting event or concert can feel a lot like traveling, with large crowds and extensive security lines, plus a laundry list of essential items to bring for a pleasant experience — all of which you have to fit in a teeny bag, or you won’t get where you want to go. I often find myself, both at stadiums and airports, asking, “What does a girl have to do just to have some fun?”

The answer — at least, for stadium events in 2026 such as the [FIFA Club World Cup](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/club-world-cup/usa-2025/stadiums/bag-policy) — is to own a small, clear bag. [Established by the NFL](https://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/21/sports/football/nfl-fans-met-by-tighter-bag-policy.html) in 2013, clear-bag policies have become the norm in stadiums across the US and Canada. The bags must be [smaller than 12 by 6 by 12 inches](https://www.nfl.com/legal/clear-bag-policy). Most importantly, they must be mostly clear and lack obstructions such as buckles or decor.

Alternatively, you can use a small clutch or wristlet, defined as smaller than 4.5 by 6.5 inches, which doesn’t actually hold that much stuff. You could also use a clear 1-gallon freezer bag, which is sort of a pain to carry around. (The size restrictions are not universal and can vary depending on the stadium, so before attending any event, it’s best to check the venue’s policy.)

The main problem is that such stadium-friendly bags are almost universally ugly. Made from clear plastic, they’re usually cheap-looking, misshapen, and largely useless for any scenario other than letting security scan through your junk. Because of their specific purpose, they can be equated to single-use plastic.

So, I went on a journey to find a stylish, practical, stadium-friendly bag that was also versatile enough to add to my day-to-day lifestyle.

To find some options that could work without offending my sensibilities, I first searched online and then shopped in stores to confirm availability, during which I discovered that many of these niche bags are offered online only. From an initial list of 25 bags, I narrowed my selection to nine styles ranging in price from $15 to $150, which I tested over two weeks and then gave to testers of various ages, sizes, and mobilities. I also spoke with [Ajoy Sarkar](https://www.fitnyc.edu/creative-nexus/faculty/directory/sarkar-ajoy.php), a textiles expert and professor at the Fashion Institute of Technology, to learn more about the category.

Style writer Frances Solá-Santiago testing various stadium bags. Frances Solá-Santiago and Erica Solano/NYT Wirecutter

My research paid off. It is possible to buy a handsome, functional clear bag that will not end up crumpled in some corner of your closet (or worse, in the trash), though they are scarce. Here are the four stadium bags I recommend.

## What I’d choose

### An elegant hard-shell crossbody

The [Away Stadium Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Dick's%20Sporting%20Goods/81517/239703/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) is everything a stadium bag should be: sturdy, practical, easy to carry, and capable of protecting your cargo. But it’s also everything that most of these bags aren’t: stylish, chic, and versatile beyond the stadium.

Available with five strap colors, the Away Stadium Bag is made from the same hard-shell polycarbonate that the company uses for [its popular luggage](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/reviews/are-away-carry-ons-worth-the-hype/). The Stadium Bag has a smooth zipper opening and a thick crossbody strap that extends to 53 inches. I was able to carry much more than its 5-by-3-by-7-inch size suggests, including a phone, a card holder, keys, sunglasses, and a lip balm.

Someone stowing a digital camera in a The Away Stadium Bag.

Because of this bag’s hard exterior, it can get noisy as the contents rattle around and hit the casing, but the sound didn’t bother me, and it probably wouldn’t be an issue in loud crowds. The bag will show wear and tear, much like its luggage counterparts, but in the two weeks I tested it (and smacked it around), it acquired no major scratches or denting.

Perhaps most importantly, it can work for a variety of other purposes too. By simply unhooking the straps, you can easily transform it into a travel case for toiletries, makeup, skin-care items, or other essentials that are prone to spillage, so it’s a perfect companion for out-of-town events. I was able to pack my usual travel skin-care and makeup routine in this bag with some room to spare. And once you’re home, you can use it as an everyday case for makeup or commuting essentials.

## What I’d skip

Of the 25 models I first reviewed, only nine made it to actual testing. Four made the cut; the rest I wouldn’t recommend. Here’s why.

- The [Mark & Graham Clear Convertible Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Mark%20%26%20Graham/81534/239365/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) was sophisticated and chic, and the company offers three-character customizations for an additional $17. But its price was too high in comparison with similar styles made of comparable materials. Plus, I was let down by the junky zipper, which was too finicky to work in a stadium setting.
- While the [Target Game Day Clear Crossbody Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Target/81535/239366/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) had a decent strap and color offerings, the material creased easily and lacked structure. Even in light of the low price of $15, I was not impressed, and I preferred other models in the same price range.
- The [Béis Stadium Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Beis%20Travel/81536/239367/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) was an all-around disappointment. Although it was the largest bag I tested, it has two openings on the sides of the zipper that you can’t close, so I did not think it would keep my stuff safe in a stadium environment. - The [Lululemon Everywhere Belt Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Lululemon/81537/239368/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) is truly everywhere at this point. - Although the [Bogg Stadium Bag](https://www.nytimes.com/wirecutter/out/affiliate/v1/Dick's%20Sporting%20Goods/81538/239720/4/link/632ff1a87bc54ba9a2e309736cbd5dae/?dp=eyJyZWYiOiJ3d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8ifQ) is a good belt bag, our larger testers were not able to strap it comfortably around their waist or hips. ## What to consider before buying a stadium bag

Most stadium bags are made from plastic, and that brings its own unique set of benefits and challenges. “It is by no means a natural material,” said textile expert Ajoy Sarkar. “Aesthetically, it is not the most pleasing, but functionally, it is one of the best.”

That’s because plastic-based materials are hard to tear and pierce, so your bag should be able to withstand the pressure and forceful movements that are typical in stadium settings. It should also protect your essentials from liquid spills (such as drinks and rain), which can happen often at these events. And because plastic is cheap to manufacture, one of these bags really shouldn’t be too expensive to buy, either.

Cleaning the bag should be straightforward. You’ll most likely be able to wipe off stains, dirt, or spills with a cloth (or your hands, when you’re in a hurry) without leaving behind any noticeable traces. Depending on the other materials used in the bag, you might also be able to wash it in a sink or bathtub.

But it still requires some maintenance. If your bag gets stained, clean it right away to prevent any long-lasting discoloration. You should also store it in a cool, shaded place, since the plastic material can crack or acquire yellow marks if exposed to heat or sun for long periods of time. To maintain the shape, try stuffing it before storing it.

Attending a game or concert should be fun. And a clear bag can eliminate the hassle of overthinking the security line. It just so happens that you can comply with those security policies in style, too.
